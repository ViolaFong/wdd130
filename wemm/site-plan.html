Overview

Purpose

WEMM, the Woodland Ecumenical and Multi-faith Ministries council, is an interfaith committee in Woodland. While we’ve been in existence well over a decade, we are still largely unknown in the community. Our goals are: 1) To increase our visibility in Woodland. 2) Invite other churches and faith communities to join us, 3) Invite the community to participate with us in service and celebration.

Audience

Our audience is: 1) The general citizenry of Woodland, CA, 2) Those inclined to faith, and those who are not, 3) Leaders and members of faith communities in this area - both those who are already WEMM members and those who are not, 4) Woodland community influencers. What do they want? 1) To know what WEMM is and what it stands for, 2) To know what WEMM is doing or has done in the community and the kinds of things we help with, 3) To know how they can join our events and contribute to our initiatives, 4) To know how their faith community can join our committee. How will our audience access the site? Via desktop, labtop, and/or mobile devices.

Content

Home

The Woodland Ecumenical and Multi-faith Ministries (WEMM) council is a group of faith communities in Woodland who meet together each month to:

• Do Good by bringing our members and the community together to serve, and by supporting local charities helping those in need, and • Promote interfaith worship, friendship, understanding, and respect.

Each participating faith community may have up to 3 voting members of WEMM. We meet once a month, rotating locations between the various member faith communities. All of Woodland’s faith communities are invited to be members of WEMM, including those serving Woodland residents. For more information, or to join one of our meetings to check it out, contact WEMM’s secretary, Viola Fong, violafong2020@gmail.com, or 530-379-3553.

Mission Statement

PURPOSE, MISSION & VISION 1. The purpose of WEMM shall be to call together diverse faith communities to foster unity, service, and fellowship. 2. The mission of WEMM shall be to a. Celebrate and cultivate the diverse faiths and cultures within the community b. Develop and support interfaith and ecumenical worship and learning opportunities c. Serve the poor, oppressed and the needy for the sake of building bridges of hope between faith communities and persons in need d. Advocate for the full humanity and inherent value of all persons e. Model and encourage the responsible stewardship of the planet and her resources. 3. The Vision of WEMM is to be and become the Beloved Community.

Doing Good

Our WEMM community loves to combine our efforts to do more good. Whether we're putting together Christmas food baskets, supporting local non-profits through the 3rd Thursday Humanitarian Day, collecting needed supplies for those in need, or doing projects to support and beautify Woodland, you're invited to join us.

Each year, WEMM puts together about 300 Christmas baskets with turkeys, potatoes, gravy, canned vegetables, stuffing, jello, and more to serve needy families, primarily through Fourth & Hope.

WEMM is a major sponsor of the 3rd Thursday Humanitarian Day, held the 3rd Thursday of each month at the church on the corner of Pioneer and Gum. The whole community is invited! Come for whatever time you have available between the hours of 1-9 p.m. and help with a variety of humanitarian projects that benefit those in need in our community, our county, and beyond. More info on JustServe.org. Here are some of the things we've done or are working on: * Wrapping diapers for the Yolo Diaper Bank (over 30,000 so far) * Packing lunches for Empower Yolo * Assembled bathroom kits for the 61 new tiny homes at Fourth & Hope * Assembled bathrooms-to-go and kitchens-to-go for Empower Yolo * Tracing, cutting, sewing, and serging liners (800+ so far) and pouches(250+ so far) for Days for Girls (reuseable feminine hygiene) * Conflict resolution seminar presented by the Yolo Conflict Resolution Center * Sewing and filling comfort bags for foster children complete with toiletry bags and sock monkeys * Food boxes for refugees * Crocheting and knitting blankets for those without a home * Rock-a-Smile

WEMM supports volunteerism! Check out the local and global opportunities to volunteer at www.justserve.org

Interfaith Events

WEMM believes that rubbing shoulders with others in the community who may believe differently than we do helps all of us develop more tolerance, understanding, and appreciation for each other. Many of our activities are designed for that purpose. There is so much good being done by so many, no matter what their background or belief may be. We want to celebrate the good in all of us.

Peace Picnic – Our last Peace Picnic was held on https://www.dailydemocrat.com/2022/09/16/community-invited-to-annual-interfaith-peace-picnic/ "We can only make it together - we can only be safe together. We can be prosperous only together. We can survive only together. We can be human only together." These are the words of the late South African Anglican Bishop Desmond Tutu, referring to the struggle against apartheid. But the words could just as easily be spoken of here and now, of Woodland in 2022. When the Muslim community in Woodland and throughout the United States felt unsafe in the aftermath of 9/11, rather than stay stuck in that feeling of victimhood, they realized that they would only be able to feel safe and survive if they reached out to others in order to build a sense of togetherness. From that effort, the annual Interfaith Community Peace Picnic was born. The Peace Picnic, to be held on Saturday, September 24th this year, 11 am to 3:30 pm at Ferns Park (750 West Southwood Drive), is designed to put into action the American Muslim Voice Foundation’s motto “From fear to friendship”. All community members – individuals, families, groups, are invited to join in this event as we rub shoulders with our neighbors and see past our differences to our common humanity. The event is free. And, of course, there will be food! The Woodland Mosque & Islamic Center sponsors the lunch of restaurant-catered Pakistani Food. The American Lutheran Church provides beverages, and all are invited (but not required) to bring a salad or dessert to add to the culinary experience. The Peace Picnic is a collaborative effort of the American Muslim Voice, the Woodland Ecumenical and Multi-faith Ministries (WEMM), and the Woodland Mosque & Islamic Center. All three organizations seek to promote interfaith harmony and community harmony. There will be games for the children, and music to enjoy besides the great food. However, the main goal is to spend time in the same space, finding out that no matter what obvious or subtle differences we may have, we also have much in common. No matter what makes you unique or the same, we hope you will join with us and we’ll all be a little more “human only together”.

This year's WEMM Thanksgiving Service will be held on November 20th, 4 pm, at the Woodland Christian Church.
